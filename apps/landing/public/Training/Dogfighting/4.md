# 4. Settings that matter

**Focus:** Combat settings so the game doesn't fight you: brake ≠ boost, gear limits off, G-Safe off, TVI/pips on, decoupled default.

**Why it matters:** Wrong settings waste boost, hide your vector, or cap your G's. Fix them once and fly with full control.

**From the ship / before you leave**  
Make sure boost and space brake are on separate bindings, and that you can toggle decoupled (see lesson 6). Find the keybind for the TVI and set it to always visible if it isn't already.

**Main menu / options**  
- **Automatic slowdown / landing gear limits** — off. Gear down shouldn't cap your speed or G-forces in combat.  
- **Space brake enables boost** — off. Brake and boost must be separate so you don't burn boost when you're just trying to slow down.  
- **G-Safe** — off. G-Safe limits how many G's the ship pulls; off gives you full acceleration when you need it.  
- **TVI** — always visible. You need to see your direction of travel at all times.  
- **Lead pip / pips** — always visible when you have a target.  
- **Camera effects** — turn off anything that hurts your aim: pilot look-ahead, camera shake, weapon recoil shake.

**Decoupled** — set decoupled as your default flight mode for combat. We cover what that means in lesson 6; the setting is so you start in the right mode when you spawn.

**Why boost matters** — In a fight, running out of boost (fatigue) means you can't dodge or escape. Fatigue gets you killed. So we don't waste boost on braking; save it for when you need it.

**Checkpoint:** You've changed these settings and you know why each one matters (HUD visible, brake ≠ boost, full control).
