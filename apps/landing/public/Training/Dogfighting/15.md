# 15. Disengage

**Focus:** When to break off a fight and how: turn away, build distance, go to NAV, boost out. Surviving is a win.

**Why it matters:** Staying when you're out of boost, low on hull, or outnumbered gets you killed. Disengage is a decision, not a defeat.

**When to disengage**  
- Low on **boost** (fatigue) — you can't dodge or run.  
- Low on **hull** — one more pass can kill you.  
- **Outnumbered** and separated — you're not helping your wingman by dying.  
- Need to **reset** — new angle, regen shields, or escape a bad position.

**How**  
1. **Turn away** from the fight — don't keep presenting your nose; break their line of fire.  
2. **Build distance** — strafe and throttle so you're extending; watch delta go negative.  
3. **NAV and boost out** — switch to NAV, boost. You're now in travel mode; get to safety or re-engage when you're ready.

Don't wait until you're dead. If you know you're losing the rate fight or you're out of boost, disengage early. In 2v1 or when separated, surviving so your team can regroup is a win.

**Checkpoint:** You can say when you would disengage (boost, hull, numbers) and you've practised the sequence: turn away, build distance, NAV, boost.
