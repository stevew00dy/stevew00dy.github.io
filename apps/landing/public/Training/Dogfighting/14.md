# 14. The turning duel and evasion

**Focus:** The rate fight: both ships orbiting, matching turn rates, trying to get and keep the nose on target. Nose authority, pip neutralise, and when to evade or disengage.

**Why it matters:** This is the core of a 1v1 — who can hold or win the turning contest. If you don't understand rate and nose authority, you're just hoping.

Once you're in range, the fight becomes a **rate fight**: both ships orbiting, pips in pitch, matching turn rates. *Rate* here means **turn rate** — how fast each ship can put its nose on the other. Roll is fastest, then pitch, then yaw (lesson 3); so you roll to put the target in your pitch axis, then pitch onto them. The duel is a contest of turn rate and position. Whoever gets their nose on target first has **nose authority** — they lead the other pilot around. **Pre-nose** means turning your nose toward the target (or where they're going) early, so you're already on or near target when it matters — anticipation instead of reacting late. It's how you get and keep nose authority.

**Pip neutralise** — you learned neutralisation in lesson 11: strafing so your vector matches the target's (pip over their ship) lets you close or hold position. Strafing opposite the pip = extending. Use it to control range and angle.

**Active evasion** — when they're on you before you're on them: roll the other way, strafe, and try to get back on top. Don't yaw; roll then pitch is faster. One option is **trajectory cutting** (lesson 10): instead of following their orbit, cut across to where they're going — you get to the "end of the circle" before they do. Get your pip on them and hold it. If you can't recover, create separation and disengage (lesson 15). **Too much circle strafe** — if you're constantly orbiting without neutralising, you push yourself away; they can hold position and then push in when you're extended or out of ammo. Neutralise first, then move.

**Knife fight** — very close range, or dodging by closing in rather than creating distance. Still a rate fight, just at short range; same principles (roll then pitch, pip, nose authority), tighter and faster.

**Checkpoint:** You can describe a rate fight and nose authority; you've practised keeping the pip on target and know when to evade or disengage instead of tunnelling.
