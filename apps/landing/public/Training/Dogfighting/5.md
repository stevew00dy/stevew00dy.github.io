# 5. Arena Commander — where and how to practice

**Focus:** AC is where you drill without cost or travel; same flight model as the PU. Know where to find it and how to set up before you fly.

**Why it matters:** Most pilots build muscle memory in AC, then take those skills into the verse. No aUEC lost, no claim times — just fly and fight.

Arena Commander (AC) is the place to drill without losing aUEC or gear. We use it for three reasons: **instant action** (no travel time, no consequences), **no cost and no grief** (respawn and try again), and **the same flight model** (what you learn here applies in the verse). You need to know how to get in and what to pick.

**Where to find it**  
From the main menu: **Game Modes** (or **Electronic Access** in some patches). Arena Commander is a separate mode from the persistent universe (PU). You pick a mode, pick a ship, and spawn straight into the action. No travel, no quantum, no claim times — just fly and fight.

**Modes that matter for training**  
- **Free Flight** — empty space (or a simple map). No enemies. Use it for 6DOF, decoupled, orbit drill, and tracking drill without pressure.  
- **Single-player vs AI** — Vanduul or pirates. Good for warm-up and practising pips and merges against predictable targets.  
- **Multiplayer** — Battle Royale (free-for-all), Squadron Battle (team deathmatch), or other modes depending on patch. Real PvP; queue times vary by region and time of day.

**Setup**  
Before you load in, have your combat settings done (lesson 4): decoupled default, TVI and pips always on, brake ≠ boost, G-Safe off. Pick a ship you're learning (e.g. a light fighter). Spawn in, confirm you're in decoupled and your HUD shows speed, TVI, and boost bar. If nobody is in multiplayer, use Free Flight or single-player to keep drilling — the mechanics are the same.

**No cost** — repairs and reclaims in AC don't use aUEC. Die as much as you need to build muscle memory, then take those skills into the PU when you're ready.

**Checkpoint:** You've loaded into Arena Commander (Free Flight or a match), know where to find it from the main menu, and have confirmed your settings and HUD before flying.
