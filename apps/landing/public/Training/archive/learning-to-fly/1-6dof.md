# 1. Six degrees of freedom (6DOF)

**Description:** How your ship moves and turns in space. Three ways to move, three ways to turn.

**Image:** /lesson-thumbnails/flying-1.svg  
**Video:** https://youtu.be/vnkJymjOpf4

---

Your ship has six degrees of freedom — three ways to move and three ways to turn.

**Movement (translation):** Forward/backward (throttle and retro). Left/right (called strafe left and strafe right — sliding sideways without turning). Up/down (strafe up, strafe down).

**Turning (rotation):** Pitch — nose goes up or down (like nodding). Yaw — nose goes left or right (like shaking your head). Roll — the ship spins around its forward axis (barrel roll).

In space, where you're pointing and where you're going can be completely different. You can fly forward while your nose points sideways. That's what makes space flight different from a car or a plane, and it's the foundation of everything else in this section.

Practice in Arena Commander — a practice mode inside Star Citizen with instant action, no travel, and no cost if you die. Select it from the main menu.

**Checkpoint:** You can name all six axes and feel the difference between strafing (moving) and turning.
