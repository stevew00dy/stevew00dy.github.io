# 7. Glossary — terms you'll hear

**Description:** Quick reference for flight and combat terms used in this section and by other pilots.

**Image:** /lesson-thumbnails/flying-7.svg  
**Video:** https://youtu.be/vnkJymjOpf4

---

**6DOF** — Six degrees of freedom: three ways to move (throttle/retro, strafe L/R, strafe U/D) and three ways to turn (pitch, yaw, roll).

**SCM / NAV** — SCM = Space Combat Maneuvers (weapons, shields, capped speed). NAV = Navigation (fast travel, weapons and shields off). Stay in SCM during a fight.

**PIP** — Predicted impact point. The marker on your HUD showing where to aim to hit a moving target.

**TVI** — Your velocity indicator (direction you're actually travelling). The target's TVI shows their direction.

**Delta** — Relative velocity between you and a target. Positive = closing. Negative = separating.

**Merge** — Closing into engagement range. The moment you commit to the fight.

**Rate fight** — The sustained turning duel when two ships orbit each other, matching turn rates, pips in pitch. The "binary circle" that most skilled fights settle into.

**Nose authority** — Who gets their nose on target first after a merge. The pilot with nose authority controls the fight.

**Speed wall** — Max speed in SCM without boost (ship-dependent). Stay under it for full maneuverability. On or above it, your acceleration in other directions gets cut.

**Rule of halves** — Effective engagement range is roughly half your weapon's velocity. E.g. a gun with 1800 m/s projectile speed → effective at ~900 m (shots land in half a second). Outside that, evasion is too easy.
