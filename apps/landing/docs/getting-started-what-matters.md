# What matters for someone getting started in Star Citizen

**Assumption:** They’ll do (or have done) the in-game tutorial — home location, basic systems, fly to a space station. So "Getting started" should **add** what the tutorial doesn’t cover or only touches on, and **reinforce** one clear path so they don’t bounce off.

---

## 1. Death and recovery (so you don’t quit)

- **What actually happens when you die** — respawn at a hospital/hab; you may lose carried gear; your ship is still yours.
- **Where you respawn** — primary residence or last-visited hospital. So…
- **Setting your spawn (regen point)** — how to set where you wake up (e.g. at a space station you use often) so you’re not stuck somewhere you hate or far from your ship.
- **Getting your ship back** — ASOP: Retrieve vs Claim. When to expedite. One mental checklist: respawn → get to ASOP → claim/retrieve → go again.

*Why it matters:* One bad death or “I’m stuck and I don’t know where my ship is” is when people quit. Remove that panic.

---

## 2. Finding your way (so you’re not lost)

- **MobiGlas** — what it is (F1), main apps: starmap, contracts, journal, personal inner thought. Where to find missions and your route.
- **Starmap (F2)** — set destination, quantum jump. One simple route to learn first (e.g. home city → orbital station → home).
- **Orienting after spawn** — “Where am I? Where’s the spaceport? Where’s ASOP?” Landmarks, signs, asking for directions in chat if needed.

*Why it matters:* Tutorial gets them to a station once. They need to be able to do it again on their own and know where things are in the UI.

---

## 3. One repeatable loop (so you feel progress)

- **One successful flight** — take off, fly somewhere, land or dock, return. No combat, no profit target. Prove you can do A→B.
- **Then one repeatable activity** — one box delivery, one bunker, one mining run, one simple bounty. Same loop a few times until it feels familiar.
- **Don’t try to do everything** — pick one thing, get good at that loop, then branch.

*Why it matters:* Tutorial is linear. The game opens up and feels huge. One loop gives a “home base” of competence.

---

## 4. Tools that actually help

- **In-game** — mobiGlas, contracts, journal, starmap. Maybe inner thought / interaction (so they can use terminals, open doors, etc.).
- **Out-of-game (our tools)** — when they’re useful: Loadout Planner before a bunker, Armour Tracker for gear, SPViewer when they care about ship performance, Regolith/UEX when they try mining or trading. Don’t overwhelm: “When you’re ready to do X, here’s a tool that helps.”

*Why it matters:* New players don’t know what they don’t know. Point them at one or two tools at the moment they need them (e.g. “Planning a bunker? Use the Loadout Planner.”).

---

## 5. Money and what to focus on

- **How to make aUEC (overview)** — missions (delivery, bunker, bounty, etc.), mining, trading, salvage. One sentence each so they know the landscape.
- **Choosing one thing to focus on first** — “Do you want to shoot, fly, mine, or trade?” Then: one activity, one loop, get comfortable. Not “learn everything.”
- **First profession or money-making activity** — suggest a low-barrier option (e.g. box delivery or one bunker type) and name one slightly harder step (e.g. bunkers, then bounties, then mining) so they have a path.

*Why it matters:* “What do I do?” is the other big bounce point. Give a simple map: one starting activity, one “next step,” and permission to stick with one thing.

---

## 6. Optional but valuable

- **Chat and community** — where to ask questions (in-game, Discord, etc.). One sentence.
- **Settings that bite new players** — e.g. boost on brake, landing gear flight limits (we cover in Flying). Mention that “some settings will matter once you start flying for real.”
- **Insurance and claims** — very short: you don’t lose the ship forever; claim times and expedite. Enough to avoid “I lost my ship” panic.
