# 11. Reading SPViewer — ship stats explained

**Description:** What the numbers mean so you can compare ships and fly smarter.

**Image:** /lesson-thumbnails/flying-11.svg  
**Video:** https://youtu.be/vnkJymjOpf4

---

SPViewer (spviewer.eu) is a community tool that shows real ship data. Two blocks matter for flying:

**FLIGHT PERFORMANCES**
- Mode toggling — how long it takes to switch between SCM and NAV. The extra seconds noted are quantum drive spool time.
- SCM / Forward Boost — your speed wall (max combat speed, e.g. 262 m/s) and max speed when holding boost (e.g. 610 m/s).
- NAV — max speed in travel mode (weapons and shields are off at this speed).
- Boost Ramp Up / Ramp Down — how fast boost activates and drops (seconds).
- Pitch / Yaw / Roll — rotation speed in degrees per second. Roll is usually highest, then pitch, then yaw. This is why we roll first and pitch second.
- Boosted — the same three rotation speeds when you're using boost. Shows how much faster you can track with boost held.

**ACCELERATIONS (in G)**
- Main — forward thrust (sustained speed and peak burst).
- Retro — backward thrust (braking power).
- Up / Down — vertical strafe strength.
- Strafe — left/right strafe strength.

Many ships have strong upward thrust — that's why "put them above you and strafe up" is effective. Use SPViewer to compare ships before you fly or buy.

**Checkpoint:** You can look up any ship, find its speed wall, rotation rates, and strafe strengths, and know what they mean.
