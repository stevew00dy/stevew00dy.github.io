# 2. Combat settings and the HUD

**Description:** Combat-specific settings and what to read on the advanced HUD. Separate from the basic settings in First Steps.

**Image:** /lesson-thumbnails/flying-2.svg  
**Video:** https://youtu.be/8XVItXxSd-Q

---

First Steps covered basic comfort settings. This lesson covers combat-specific settings and the HUD elements you need for fighting.

Use the Advanced HUD (Options → HUD configuration) so you see everything: mode, speed, throttle, G meter, boost bar. Turn off camera effects that work against you in a fight (pilot look-ahead, camera shake, weapon recoil shake).

**Settings to change:**
1. Automatic slowdown / landing gear limits — off. Gear down shouldn't cap your speed or G-forces.
2. Space brake enables boost — off. Brake and boost must be separate inputs so you don't waste boost when slowing down.
3. G safe — off. G safe limits how many G-forces the ship pulls to protect the pilot; turning it off gives you full acceleration.
4. TVI (velocity indicator) — set to always visible. The TVI is a marker on your HUD showing which direction your ship is actually travelling.
5. Lead pip, pips always visible. The pip (predicted impact point) shows where to aim to hit a moving target.

**What to read on the HUD:** Mode — SCM (combat: weapons on, shields on, speed capped) vs NAV (travel: fast, weapons/shields off). Speed — your current speed in m/s. TVI — your direction of travel. Delta — when you target another ship, delta shows how fast you're closing or separating. Boost bar — your afterburner fuel; when it's empty, you can't dodge or run.

**Checkpoint:** You have the Advanced HUD on, combat settings fixed, and you can point at mode, speed, TVI, delta, and boost on the screen.
