# 1. Introduction to dogfighting

## What is dogfighting

**Dogfighting** is ship combat at close range — two pilots trying to kill each other. On the surface it looks simple. Face a skilled pilot and you start to see patterns: positioning, timing, when they commit, when they break off. That depth is probably why you're here — to understand it and become a pilot who can hold their own in the verse.

---

## Why it matters

In Star Citizen, combat is everywhere. Bounties, piracy, org ops, or just holding your own when someone picks a fight. 

You'll face both players and AI — the same foundations apply. Even if you're not a "combat main," understanding dogfighting keeps you alive and expands what you can do. You don't have to love it to need it; if you do, we give you the foundations to build from.

---

## What are the main foundations?

The first stage, where possible, is **identification** — know what you're fighting. Strategies like isolating an enemy or engaging 2v1 matter. We keep it simple for this guide and focus on a typical 1v1 scenario where both pilots know they are fighting. 

**Duel etiquette:** when both pilots consent to a fight, the convention is to **pass and flare** before engaging — a fly-by, then flares as a signal you're ready. Not everyone does it; when they do, it's a fair start.

The main stages of a fight are **the merge**, **the duel**, **the disengage**. That's your map. We go into what each stage means later; for now, know they exist. 

We build the basics first (controls, HUD, settings, drills) and then work through each stage.
