# 11. Neutralisation

**Focus:** Pip neutralisation is strafing so your vector matches the target's — pip on their ship. You use it to close, hold position, or extend. It's the foundation for controlling range and getting shots on target.

**Why it matters:** Without neutralisation, you're either chasing the pip randomly or flying in a way that keeps you extending (pip ahead of them). With it, you deliberately close, hold, or extend. Every merge and every duel uses it.

**What is neutralisation**  
**Pip neutralise** means strafing so your **vector** (direction of travel) matches the target's. When your vector matches theirs, the **pip** (predicted impact point) sits over their ship — you're on the same line of travel. You're not drifting sideways relative to them; you're going the same way they are. That's "neutral" in the sense that your relative motion is neutralised. From there you can add throttle to **close** (catch up), hold strafe to **hold position**, or strafe the other way to **extend** (pip moves ahead of them, you're pulling away).

**Why it matters in a fight**  
On the **merge** (lesson 13), you use neutralisation to close at a controlled rate — pip on them, then throttle in. In the **duel** (lesson 14), you use it to hold position in the orbit or to close into effective range when you're lagging. If you're losing, you can strafe opposite the pip to extend and buy time or disengage. So: neutralisation is how you control whether you're closing, holding, or extending. No neutralisation = no control.

**How to do it**  
Watch the pip. If the pip is **ahead** of the target (they're "in front" of the pip), you're closing — or you're on a crossing path. To **neutralise**, strafe toward where the pip is relative to the target until the pip sits over the target. Now your vector matches theirs. To **close**, add forward throttle (or strafe toward them). To **extend**, strafe so the pip moves ahead of the target — you're now moving away from their vector. **Don't rely on forward** to get into position — use strafe to get where you want to be; add a little forward only to stay close. Too much forward pushes you away from the target. In decoupled (lesson 6) you hold that vector until you change it or hit the brake. Practice in AC: lock a target (or use a fixed point and imagine the pip), strafe until the pip is on them, then add throttle or strafe the other way and watch the pip move. That's the skill.

**Checkpoint:** You can explain what pip neutralisation is (matching vector so pip is on target), and you've practised in AC: strafe to put pip on target, then close or extend.
