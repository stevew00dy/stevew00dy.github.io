# 10. Key terms

**Focus:** One place for the words pilots use: vector, TVI, delta, pip, rate, merge, nose authority, pre-nose, blow through, push and pull, trajectory cutting, speed wall, SCM/NAV, disengage, knife fight.

**Why it matters:** The rest of the path assumes you know these. Use this lesson as a reference; you don't have to memorise everything before moving on.

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Vector** | The direction (and sense) your ship is moving through space; independent of where the nose points. |
| **TVI** | Trajectory / velocity indicator — the HUD marker showing your current direction of travel (your vector). |
| **Delta** | The difference in velocity between you and your target. Shown on HUD when target locked; positive = closing, negative = separating. |
| **Pip** | Predicted impact point — where to aim so your shots hit a moving target; also shows relative motion (closing / same vector / extending). |
| **Pip neutralise** | Strafing so your vector matches the target's (pip over their ship); lets you close or hold position. Strafing opposite the pip = extending. |
| **Rate** | Turn rate — how fast you can put your nose on target. Pitch rate is usually better than yaw. |
| **Rate fight** | The turning duel: both ships orbiting, matching turn rates, pips in pitch, nose on nose. Not the same as knife fight. |
| **Merge** | Closing into effective weapon range so the fight can begin. |
| **Nose authority** | Who has their nose on target first in the duel; that pilot can lead the other around. |
| **Pre-nose** | Turning your nose toward the target (or where they're going) *before* you're in perfect position — leading with the nose so you're already on or near target when you need to be. Anticipation, not reaction; helps you get nose authority. |
| **Blow through** | Intentionally flying past the target (passing through) instead of stopping at range — you go past, then turn around and re-engage. The point is to *control* the pass: pass because you meant to, not by mistake. Opposite of accidentally overshooting. |
| **Push and pull** | You can't always be the aggressor or the defender. Sometimes you neutralise and let them come to you; sometimes you push in. Who pushes and who pulls dictates who controls the engagement. |
| **Trajectory cutting / cut the circle** | Instead of following the target's orbit, cut *across* to where they're going — the "end of the circle" before they get there. Gets you back on top when you're at a disadvantage; breaks the mutual orbit. |
| **Speed wall** | Max speed in SCM without boost (ship-specific). Above it your lateral acceleration drops. Stay under it in a fight. |
| **SCM / NAV** | SCM = Space Combat Maneuvers (weapons, shields, limited speed). NAV = Navigation (fast, no weapons/shields). |
| **Disengage** | Breaking off the fight: turn away, build distance, go to NAV, boost out. |
| **Knife fight** | Very close range, or a style where you dodge by closing in rather than creating distance. Distinct from "rate fight" — the rate fight is the orbit duel; knife fight is when that duel (or any fight) is at very close range. |

**Checkpoint:** You can define vector, TVI, delta, pip, rate fight, merge, nose authority, pre-nose, blow through, push and pull, trajectory cutting, and disengage without looking.
