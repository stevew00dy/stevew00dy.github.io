# 13. The merge

**Focus:** Closing into effective weapon range the right way: delta, when to be in SCM, and common mistakes. How you merge sets up the duel.

**Why it matters:** A bad merge (too fast, wrong mode, or no awareness of delta) puts you at a disadvantage before the turning fight even starts.

The **merge** is closing into effective weapon range so the fight can begin. The moment you're in range and committed. How you do it matters.

**Delta** — the difference in velocity between you and the target (positive = closing, negative = separating). On the HUD when target locked. You want to close at a controlled rate; **around 200 m/s delta** is a common target so you're not overshooting or coming in so slow they can dictate. Adjust for ship and situation. Neutralisation (lesson 11) is how you control that — strafe to put the pip on them to close or hold.

**NAV merge** — some pilots approach in NAV (fast) then switch to SCM as they close. Lets you get to the fight quickly; you must be in SCM before you're in weapon range so you have weapons and shields. **Delta merge** — closing in SCM the whole way, watching delta and pip. Good for learning and when you're already close.

**SCM in the fight** — once you're in range, stay in SCM. Don't hit NAV by accident; that disables weapons and shields. Stay under your **speed wall** (lesson 12) so lateral acceleration stays strong.

**Common mistakes** — coming in too fast (overshoot, can't get nose on); staying in NAV into range (no guns); ignoring delta (no idea if you're closing or extending); firing too early (waste ammo, give away position). Hold fire until effective range (typically under ~800 m; much better damage closer in). If you're going to pass the target, make it deliberate — a **blow through** (past them, then turn around); don't pass by mistake (lesson 10).

**Checkpoint:** You can close to a target watching delta and pip, switch to SCM before range, and say what you'd do differently after a bad merge.
